# RatedBy

**Trust infrastructure for peer-to-peer social commerce.**

[getratedby.com](https://getratedby.com) · Built with Opus 4.7  
Cerebral Valley x Anthropic Hackathon, April 2026

---

## The problem

Global social commerce is a $2 trillion market growing toward 
$8 trillion by 2030. It runs almost entirely without trust 
infrastructure.

500 million people buy from strangers on Instagram, TikTok, 
Facebook, Facebook Marketplace, WhatsApp, WhatsApp Business, 
and LinkedIn every day. No verified identity. No complaint 
history. No fraud detection. Just a profile, a price, and a prayer.

The seller looks legitimate. The photos are real. The money moves. 
The goods never arrive.

---

## What RatedBy does

Enter any vendor's social handle, phone number, or business name.  
Get a **Trust Index** (0–100) in under 10 seconds.

**Supported platforms**  
Instagram · TikTok · Facebook · Facebook Marketplace  
WhatsApp · WhatsApp Business · LinkedIn

---

## How it works

**1. Public signal extraction**  
Every social profile leaves a fingerprint — account age, posting 
consistency, engagement authenticity, cross-platform identity, 
handle history. RatedBy reads these automatically.

**2. Crowdsourced complaint intelligence**  
Buyers submit structured reports. Not free-text reviews — 
structured intake that extracts what happened, how payment moved, 
and what patterns emerged.

**3. AI-native fraud pattern recognition**  
Opus 4.7 classifies fraud type — bait-and-switch, fee escalation, 
handle migration, payment diversion — and resolves whether handles 
across platforms belong to the same entity. This requires reasoning 
that rules engines cannot replicate.

**4. Network graph analysis**  
Vendors share infrastructure: phone numbers, payment accounts, 
product photos. The graph detects when a clean vendor connects 
to flagged entities, surfacing fraud rings invisible to 
single-profile analysis.

**5. Trust Index output**  
A score (0–100), a letter grade, and three plain-language risk 
flags. Mathematical output of aggregated signals — not editorial 
judgment.
